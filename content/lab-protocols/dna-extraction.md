## Overview
This protocol describes the standard phenol-chloroform method for extracting high-quality genomic DNA from biological samples.

## Materials
- Tissue sample
- TEN buffer (10 mM Tris-HCl, 1 mM EDTA, 0.15 M NaCl)
- Proteinase K
- Phenol/Chloroform/Isoamyl alcohol (25:24:1)
- Chloroform/Isoamyl alcohol (24:1)
- Isopropanol
- 70% Ethanol
- TE buffer

## Procedure

### Step 1: Sample Preparation
1. Weigh tissue sample (approximately 100-500 mg)
2. Add 1 mL TEN buffer per 100 mg tissue

### Step 2: Protein Digestion
1. Add Proteinase K (final concentration 100 µg/mL)
2. Incubate at 50°C for 2-3 hours with gentle agitation
3. For complete digestion, can extend overnight

### Step 3: Phenol Extraction
1. Add equal volume of Phenol/Chloroform/Isoamyl alcohol
2. Mix gently for 5 minutes
3. Centrifuge at 3000 rpm for 10 minutes
4. Carefully transfer aqueous phase to new tube

### Step 4: Chloroform Extraction
1. Add equal volume of Chloroform/Isoamyl alcohol
2. Mix gently for 5 minutes
3. Centrifuge at 3000 rpm for 10 minutes
4. Transfer aqueous phase to new tube

### Step 5: DNA Precipitation
1. Add 2/3 volume of isopropanol
2. Mix gently to precipitate DNA
3. Spool out DNA with glass rod
4. Wash with 70% ethanol
5. Air dry

### Step 6: Resuspension
1. Resuspend in TE buffer
2. Store at 4°C overnight to fully dissolve
3. Store long-term at -20°C

## Quality Control
- Check DNA concentration using NanoDrop spectrophotometer
- Verify purity by checking A260/A280 ratio (should be 1.8)
- Run agarose gel to check for degradation

## Notes
- Handle samples gently throughout to avoid shearing
- Always use sterile, nuclease-free equipment
- Wear protective gloves when handling organic solvents
