# Short Description

One of the most common and most widely distributed subtypes. It has a very broad host range, occurring in humans worldwide, in non-human primates, in many other mammals (pigs, cattle, dogs, rodents and others) and in birds. Because of this low host specificity it is regarded as zoonotic, and it is frequently recovered from environmental water and occasionally from soil or produce as faecal contamination. Together with ST2-ST4 it accounts for the great majority of human infections.
