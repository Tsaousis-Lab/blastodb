Subtypes

Below you'll find descriptions for known subtypes, with downloadable resources. Contact us if we missed any.

Note on interpretation: subtype naming reflects genetic clustering and is usually determined from sequence data. Morphology alone is generally not sufficient to subtype isolates reliably.

ST1 Human Animal

Subtype 1

ST1 is among the most prevalent subtypes found in humans worldwide. It has been isolated from a wide range of hosts including primates and livestock.

ST2 Human

Subtype 2

ST2 is predominantly found in humans and has been detected across multiple continents. Associated with both symptomatic and asymptomatic carriage.

Download reference sequence →

ST3 Human Primate

Subtype 3

ST3 is the most frequently reported subtype in human studies globally. Strongly associated with human and non-human primate hosts.

Download reference sequence →

ST4 Human Europe

Subtype 4

ST4 shows a distinct geographic distribution, being especially prevalent in Europe. Rarely reported in animals other than humans.

Download reference sequence →

ST5 Animal Livestock

Subtype 5

ST5 is predominantly an animal subtype, commonly detected in pigs, cattle, and other livestock. Occasional zoonotic transmission has been reported.

Download reference sequence →

ST6 Animal Bird

Subtype 6

ST6 is mainly detected in birds and less frequently in humans. Its epidemiological significance in human disease is still under investigation.

Download reference sequence →

ST7 Animal Bird

Subtype 7

ST7 has been found in birds and occasionally in humans. Research into its pathogenic potential is ongoing.
